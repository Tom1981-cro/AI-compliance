# EU AI Act Requirements Summary

## Introduction
The EU AI Act establishes a framework for ensuring that artificial intelligence systems used within the European Union are safe and respect fundamental rights.

## Key Requirements
1. **Risk-Based Classification**:
   - AI systems are classified into different risk categories: unacceptable risk, high risk, limited risk, and minimal risk.

2. **High-Risk Systems**:
   - Must comply with stringent requirements in design, testing, and use.
   - Require an assessment of compliance before deployment.

3. **Transparency Obligations**:
   - Users must be informed when they interact with AI systems.
   - Certain AI systems must provide clear information about their capabilities.

4. **Human Oversight**:
   - High-risk AI systems must ensure human oversight to prevent or minimize risks to health, safety, and fundamental rights.

5. **Data Quality Requirements**:
   - High-risk AI systems must use high-quality data to train algorithms and mitigate biases.

6. **Post-Market Monitoring**:
   - Continuous monitoring of AI systems after deployment to ensure compliance with EU regulations.

7. **Documentation and Record Keeping**:
   - Ensure proper documentation to provide evidence of compliance and transparency to authorities.

## Conclusion
The EU AI Act aims to foster innovation in AI while safeguarding public interest and upholding fundamental rights. Stakeholders are responsible for understanding their obligations under this legislation.
